How to Use This App

About The App

  • created by Aiman 
  • This is just a side project I embarked on to get experience on how  web design feels like. The contents are mostly from the notes that I took from Ustadh Nouman Ali Khan's lecture on Surah Al-Fatihah.  

Navigation

  • Click on any ayats on the image on the left, and the tafseer for that ayat will be revealed in the panel on the right.
  • You can also click on any blue colored buttons in the top navigation bar to reveal the tafseer of the ayat. The ayat that you chose will automatically get highlighted.

Accessibility

  • If you feel that the texts are too small for reading purposes, you can adjust the text size using the buttons at the bottom right of the panel. 
  • Click on the '+' sign to increase the text size, and '-' sign to decrease it.

Sharing

  • Click on the green 'Share' button to share this website among your social network friends. Remember to include some interesting captions and hashtags!

Feedback

  • Any suggestion is highly appreciated, really!

Ayat Tafseer

Background

  1. First complete surah given to Rasulullah ﷺ.
  2. Fatihah will open the doors to the other parts of the Quran
    1. That's why its always paired with other Surah in our prayers.
  3. All you need to know about God is the first 3 ayaats of Al-Fatihah.
  4. First 3 ayats of Fatihah is the knowledge, the rest are our testimony to action.

  1. الحمدلله

    1. We thank and praise Allah no matter what has happened, although we may not understand the wisdom behind it.
    2. ثناء - Praise
      1. Example: Praise athlete that is good
    3. Thanks
      1. When someone does something for you
      2. When someone does you a favor
    4. When you praise something, it doesn't necessarily mean that you will thank it.
    5. When you thank someone, you don't necessarily have to praise it.
      1. Musa as thanks Firaun, but will never praise him for what he does.
    6. It wasn't المدح or الشكر لله
      1. Why?
        1. المدح : Fake praises are done all the time, to impress people, but was never sincere.
        2. الشكر: Thanks is only given as a reaction. It wasn't given out of the blue.
      2. الحمد can only mean genuinity and non-reactionary.
    7. The concise nature of the word conveys the best meaning. The shorter a word to describe something, the better it is.
    8. No 'و' (and) used to pair المدح and الشكر 
      1. و will separate the meaning
        1. Thanks and praise for two different things.
        2. Means sometime you are praising for something, but some other times thanking Him for something else.
      2. الحمد لله is not just something that we say, it is supposed to be an attitude that we practice
    9. الحمد لله is in the form of a noun
      1. Nouns don't have any tense - they are timeless and permanent.
      2. We are praising Allah now, but the praise and thanks to Allah will be there forever
      3. It will never depend on any person reciting it i.e. it doesn't need a doer.
      4. Acknowledging that Allah doesn't need us, we are the ones who need him.
    10. Why is it not in the form of a command?
      1. So that it was never a choice for anyone or anything to perform.
      2. The phrase never depends on Allah creations.
    11. It doesn't have an 'انَّ' (Absolutely) as usually said before Khutba
      1. Informative vs Emotional
      2. A usual sentence in Arabic can either convey feelings or deliver information.
      3. 'انَّ' limits to it being informative. Without 'انَّ' it could be both informative and emotional.
    12. Why not 'لله الحمد'?
      1. The unusual sequence of الحمد and لله will imply exclusivity.
      2. In other words, praise and thanks is ONLY for Allah.
      3. The word only is used when
        1. there is a need for emphasis (when you are in argument)
        2. for an extra meaning. (eg. الحمد cannot belong to anyone else)
      4. When 'only' is used, it can only mean that either there are some confusion, or the conversation is actually a debate that needs to be won.
      5. Allah never wanted to make it a debate, thus he never uses 'only'.
      6. It didn't need to be forced into our hearts, because its already in us.
    13. The importance of the word الله .
      1. Allah introduces himself to us with His name.
      2. If 'لله' is missing, it would cause confusion to who is the 'رب العالمىن ' (Master of the Worlds).
      3. If 'لله' is replaced by some other names of Allah, it will only mean that the الحمد is for those qualities that the name possesses.

  2. ربِّ العلمىن 

    1. رب
      1. Someone who owns.
        1. We are the property.
      2. Someone who ensures growth and takes care of it.
        1. Sometimes people own something but never takes care of it. Allah is never like that.
        2. Allah is the caretaker of
          1. The flow of our blood
          2. Our breath
          3. Everything that made use live
      3. Someone who give gifts to the thing he owns.
        1. The gifts are given besides the things that the property deserves.
        2. If we ever receive something, we should never complain, because we never own or deserve it.
      4. Someone who have the authority over his properties
        1. He has full control over His properties.
      5. All of us have no authority over anything, only Allah does.
      6. رب creates a relationship between us and Him
        1. We have a lot of relationships with Allah, but the most important is:
          1. Allah is رب, and we are عبد.
          2. Accepting this is the summary of the entire Quran.
        2. A slave have no choice.
          1. However, he cannot do anything until the master tells him what to do.
          2. A slave is a slave all the time.
          3. A slave is not the same as a servant, we don't give a service and expect a paycheck from Allah. We can't say no when there is something that we may not like.
          4. The رب and عبد relationship represents a connection to the ayat اهْدِنَا الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَقِيمَ meaning: tell me what to do Ya رب.
        3. Relation to previous phrase:
          1. We are so grateful to Allah that naturally the slavery becomes a thing of choice. We want to become slaves to Allah out of willingness and love.
          2. This is a unique form of slavery.
    2. الْعَالَمِينَ
      1. Definition: worlds of people, different nations.
      2. A world that spans the experience of everyone living.
        1. Distinct cultures
        2. Distinct civilizations
      3. Allah created all the cultures, languages and civilizations.
      4. You can live in all these different environments/worlds, and still be guided.

الرَّحْمَـٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ

  1. Both word means:
    1. Intense love
    2. Care
    3. Concern
    4. Mercy
      1. No matter how many times we messed up, Allah always let it slip through.
      2. He never sent axes flying from the sky chopping our heads when we look at haram things, no thunder comes striking when we say bad words to our fellow human beings.
  2. الرَّحْمَـٰنِ
    1. Its extreme and beyond expectation.
    2. Its happening immediately and right now.
    3. Its temporary.
    4. For this world.
  3. الرَّحِيمِ
    1. Permanent.
    2. Not necessarily right now.
    3. For the muslims in the next life.
  4. Why is الرَّحْمَـٰنِ first and الرَّحِيمِ second?
    1. We only think about the future after our immediate concern is taken care of.
    2. Allah understands us so well, that he takes care of our immediate concerns first, and subsequently guarantees mercy even in the future.

مَالِكِ يَوْمِ الدِّينِ 

  1. Puts us back in check 
  2. Master of the day of judgement 
  3. الدِّينِ: 
    1. Accurate dealings 
    2. Justice 
    3. Only two ways on judgement day: 
      1. Either mercy or justice, and justice is the bad option. 
    4. Some people will have hissaban yaseera (easy judgement) that Allah overlooks they shortcomings. 
    5. If you even get asked on judgement day, you will not end well. If the record is actually checked, you are finished. 
    6. If you take advantage of Allah's mercy, Allah doesn't take it lightly (relation to الرَّحْمَـٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ). 
  4. Balances الرَّحْمَـٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ. 
  5. Its impossible to believe in God and not believe in an afterlife 
  6. Believing in a perfect God necessitates the coming of justice in the afterlife. 
    1. A man who had killed 100 lives, how is he to be killed for justice to be served? 
    2. Someone who has stole a money he doesn't return, how is he to be judged if he was never caught in this world? 
  7. There is no absolute justice in this world, the judges of any dispute will be biased. Examples:
    1. Women and men. 
    2. Employer and employee. 
    3. But Allah is the ultimate judge, and He transcends all his creations. 
  8. Allah owns that day. 
    1. A king sees the whole thing.
    2. There are two ways to read مَالِكِ
      1. مَالِكِ - king, macro,
      2. مَلِكِ - owner, owner
    3. Judgement day: 
      1. Big things: Taken care of by the king 
      2. Small things: taken care of by the owner 
    4. Why add the word day?  
      1. Day = time 
      2. Allah owns not  just the judgement, but also the time and everything that happens within it. Things that happen across the universe, over every single second. 
  9. Two kinds of people on judgement day: 
    1. People who gets Allah mercy 
    2. People who gets Allah punishment/justice. 
      1. Punishment = Justice. 

  1. إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ

    1. This conclusion after knowing Allah in the last 3 ayats
    2. The desire to talk to him is signified by the change from third person to second person pronouns. 
    3. Slavery is usually never done willingly. 
      1. But this ayat: we are ready to be a slave. You have to make a choice. 
      2. Nobody should ever make you a Muslim. You should come to Allah by yourself. 
      3. It has to be voluntary. 
    4. I don't wanna do what I want to do anymore, I only want to do what You want me to do. 
    5. Ya Allah you have been so good to me, I am ready. Just take me. 
    6. Allah has created human beings in the kind of labor that slaves do, whether or not we want to be the slave of Allah, we will always be slaves to either other human beings, wealth or other wordly things. But the only thing that can liberate us is by becoming the slave of Allah. 
    7. If you are not a slave of Allah, you will be a slave of something else. 

  2. وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ 

    1. Its only and only Your help that we seek. 
    2. When we commit to being a slave, we need help from Allah. 
    3. Dozens of other word for help: 
      1. عون 
      2. نصر 
      3. مدد
      4. مساعدة  
    4. عون is a specific kind of help: 
      1. If you are not strong enough to do something, you ask someone to help you do it. 
      2. You have to show your part first, then the help of Allah will come. 
      3. It requires you do work for yourself, and when you got stuck, you ask help. 
      4. Ya Allah, I am trying, I am putting in the effort, you can see it, and now I need your help. 
    5. Stop thinking that everything you gain is from yourself. Success comes from your effort and Allah's help. 
    6. نَسْتَعِينُ 
      1. What are we asking for help in? 
      2. When we are so desperate to ask a help for, we just say "help". 
      3. The things that we need helping are so many we can't even list them, we can't even explain. 
      4. Other meaning: Allah knows better what you need, just ask for help, He'll take care of it. 
    7. Why إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ first and وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ second? 
      1. Purpose in this world is to enslave ourselves. 
      2. What you want for Allah (نَعْبُدُ) should be mention first, what you want for yourselves (نَسْتَعِينُ) should be mention second.

اهْدِنَا الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَقِيمَ

  1. "We are ready to do it. Show us the way". 
  2. Number one thing that you should ask, in any occasion, is guidance. 
  3. Why اهْدِنَا? 
    1. Gift. 
    2. It's the best item to give in a very specific condition. 
    3. Guide us implies that we come together as community to get guidance. 
    4. Not إيَّا نهدي: guide 'only' us. We need to have the mindset that everyone has to get that guidance.  
    5. Seeking Allah's help together. Collective life is very important.  
  4. Sometimes guidance is just information, but Allah's guidance is more than information. 
    1. Its personal choices at any given moment. 
    2. It's the strength to make the right decisions. 
    3. It's not just information because we ask Allah over and over again, information, however, is usually just conveyed once. 
    4. We say it every few hours. 
    5. Guidance is not something that we keep. Nobody can say that 'I have it, I don't need it anymore'.  
  5. Where is the إلى , بِ, لِ between اهْدِنَا and الصِّرَاطَ? 
    1. إلى: just to get the directions to a destination 
    2. لِ: hold someone's hand and take them all the way to the destination 
    3. بِ: use this 'something' to go there. 
    4. If none of these are used, then it means all of them at the same time. 
  6. Guidance is not knowledge. 
    1. You can be the most knowledgable person, but not have guidance. 
    2. Even if you are a muslim, you don't own guidance. 
    3. We should be very desperate and feel in need of it all the time, so much so that we ask for it every rakaat. 
  7. اهْدِنَا : 
    1. Guide us 
      1. To 
      2. Through 
      3. And all the way to the end of straight path. 
    2. Why guide to a path, not destination? 
      1. There is only one way to that destination. 
      2. If you get onto the road, you'll get to the destination. 
      3. The road itself will help you get there. 
    3. "I am really nervous, I need you to stay with me". 
      1. معيَّ : 
        1. Ya Allah guide me through this path, stay with me as I am travelling so I am not a traveller alone.  
        2. And also guide me all the way to end. (to Jannah) 
    4. "Ya Allah, keep me at this until death comes" 
      1. So that I don't burn out. 
    5. We have to ask this like we are really desperate, like a thirsty man in a desert needing of a water. 
  8. الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَقِيمَ 
    1. Some other words used for paths: 
      1. طريق
      2. سبيل 
      3. صِّرَاطَ
    2. صِّرَاطَ: 
      1. A road that is wide, that multiple people can travel  on it
      2. Its straight.
      3. Its a path that is dangerous. 
    3. الْمُسْتَقِيمَ : 
      1. Straight: 
      2. Why compound the meaning? 
        1. Its actually going up. 
        2. Rising to Allah 
        3. Leaving the love of this world. 
        4. Gets more dangerous when you are higher. 
      3. The more progress you make, the more prone you are to falling. 
      4. People up there worry more about falling down. People who gets further gets more concerned. 
      5. No guarantees that you can get all the way to the end. Some people may fail halfway. 
      6. There are no such things as safe place or status in Islam, not until you leave this world. 
      7. When people do crash, they crash bad. Allah protect us. 
      8. When you go higher, your view improves.  
        1. See things you never did. 
        2. Understand things you never could. 
      9. Who will help you? 
        1. People who have done it (relation is in the next ayat) 

صِرَاطَ الَّذِينَ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ 

  1. Path who you have favoured, those of the past 
  2. أَنْعَمْتَ from نعوم 
    1. Softness 
    2. Ease 
    3. Comfort 
  3. The journey hard, but Allah made it easy for these people. 
  4. They are the prophets and messengers. 
  5. The real role models of Islam are the ones who have left. 

غَيْرِ الْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا الضَّالِّينَ 

  1. But also, Allah, show me who have messed up 
  2. الْمَغْضُوبِ: 
    1. Allah is so angry that he didn't even mention himself/pronouns with the 'anger'. 
    2. Some among Jews have the kind of behaviour that can be described as مَغْضُوبِ 
    3. Its an anger that comes even after that person knows it. 
    4. Jews that Allah talked about in the Quran are the most knowledgable but they do the wrong thing anyway. 
    5. We are in danger of becoming those kind of people, the kind of people who know, but still do the wrong thing. 
    6. There is no "your" in مَغْضُوبِ like in أَنْعَمْتَ - Allah is so angry at them He doesn't even mention Himself in the phrase. 
    7. They had anger brought upon them from everyone (angels, humans and all creations). 
    8. It's a noun which implies permanence.  
    9. The مَغْضُوبِ are always there (alive) as opposed to those who had succeed. 
  3. وَلَا الضَّالِّينَ 
    1. Some among the Christians have the kind of behavior that can be described as الضَّالِّينَ. 
    2. Lost means their direction is missing. 
    3. People who do the wrong thing and doesn't even know its wrong. 
    4. But this is no excuse for us, because we are supposed to learn. 
    5. If you say you don't want to learn because then you will be guilty, then its like saying you like being the الضَّالِّينَ.
